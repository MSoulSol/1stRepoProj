If I could be any animal, I would be an elephant.

Despite their size, they have an enormous capacity for agility and nimbleness in movement. They may be fierce at times, but also capable of exercising compassion in times of distress--such as in mourning a matriarch--or in regular daily tasks like caring for the young. Finally they exhibit excellent cognitive skills. These are illustrated in a number of ways. Firstly, by phenomenal memory in geo-mapping routes for water and food, or in discerning and recalling specific relationships among members of the herd. Secondly, by critical thinking and problem solving capacities--whether it is the fashioning and usage for tools for tasks, or in the strategizing and implementation of a plan to ward off predators. The elders and older siblings also make gigantic efforts to bequeath accumulated knowledge to the young.
